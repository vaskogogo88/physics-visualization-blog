Exploring Non-linear Dynamics: From Simple Harmonics to Deterministic Chaos

Introduction

This project presents an interactive study of Pohl's torsion pendulum, utilizing a custom-built data pipeline to explore dynamic behaviors ranging from simple harmonic motion to chaotic oscillations. By analyzing the system across four distinct regimes, specifically undamped, damped, damped-driven, and chaotic, I demonstrate how incremental changes in parameters such as friction, external driving forces, and potential well configurations fundamentally alter the system's evolution.

Using Python and video tracking software, I extracted high-resolution angular displacement, velocity, and acceleration data to construct the interactive 3D phase space trajectories and Poincaré maps featured below. While the transition to a double-well potential successfully induced non-linear behavior, capturing a clear 'Butterfly Effect' proved challenging. The inherent limitations of 30 fps video tracking and environmental noise introduced enough uncertainty to mask the system's sensitive dependence on initial conditions. However, the resulting phase space trajectories still clearly demonstrate the irregular, non-repeating nature characteristic of deterministic chaos.
